Course Description

Applied Calculus is designed for students in business, economics, life sciences, and social sciences. This course introduces fundamental concepts of differential and integral calculus with emphasis on applications rather than theoretical proofs. Students will learn to model real-world problems using calculus techniques and interpret results in practical contexts.

Learning Objectives

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

Differentiation Skills

  • Calculate derivatives using basic rules and techniques
  • Apply the chain rule, product rule, and quotient rule
  • Find derivatives of exponential, logarithmic, and trigonometric functions
  • Determine implicit derivatives and related rates

Applications of Derivatives

  • Analyze rates of change in business and scientific contexts
  • Optimize functions to solve real-world problems
  • Sketch graphs using derivative information
  • Model and solve related rate problems

Integration Techniques

  • Evaluate definite and indefinite integrals
  • Apply substitution methods
  • Use integration by parts for appropriate functions

Applications of Integrals

  • Calculate areas under curves and between curves
  • Solve problems involving accumulated change
  • Apply integrals to business and life science problems
  • Understand and use the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

Multivariable Functions

  • Find partial derivatives of functions of two variables
  • Locate and classify critical points
  • Solve optimization problems with constraints

Course Outline

Unit 1: Functions and Limits (Weeks 1-2)

  • Review of functions (linear, quadratic, exponential, logarithmic)
  • Introduction to limits
  • Continuity and discontinuity
  • Applications in business and economics

Unit 2: Introduction to Derivatives (Weeks 3-4)

  • Definition of derivative as rate of change
  • Derivative notation and interpretation
  • Basic differentiation rules (power, constant, sum/difference)
  • Derivatives of exponential and logarithmic functions

Unit 3: Advanced Differentiation Techniques (Weeks 5-6)

  • Product rule and quotient rule
  • Chain rule and its applications
  • Implicit differentiation
  • Related rates problems

Unit 4: Applications of Derivatives (Weeks 7-9)

  • Critical points and extrema
  • First and second derivative tests
  • Optimization problems in business and science
  • Curve sketching and analysis
  • Marginal analysis in economics

Unit 5: Introduction to Integration (Weeks 10-11)

  • Antiderivatives and indefinite integrals
  • Basic integration rules
  • Substitution method
  • Definite integrals and the Fundamental Theorem

Unit 6: Applications of Integration (Weeks 12-13)

  • Area under curves and between curves
  • Applications to business (consumer and producer surplus)
  • Accumulated change problems
  • Average value of functions

Unit 7: Functions of Several Variables (Weeks 14-15)

  • Functions of two variables
  • Partial derivatives
  • Applications to business optimization
  • Constrained optimization (Lagrange multipliers - if time permits)

Final Exam Period (Week 16)

Attendance Policy
